Comparison Note: Prior dashboard (Apr 27, 2026 refresh) used shorter lookbacks (30-Day=1 mo, 60-Day=2 mo, 90-Day=2025 cohort n=20). This refresh uses Apr 3–May 4 (30-Day) · Mar 3–May 4 (60/90-Day) with much healthier 90-Day sample (n=93 vs. n=20 previously).
Factor Score Change — Prior vs. Current Refresh% point change
Key Movements
▲ 90-Day scores rebounded strongly — all factors improved
With a proper current cohort (n=93 vs. prior n=20 from 2025), 90-Day scores are materially stronger. Job Satisfaction jumped to 88.4%, Overall to 85.5%, Communication to 83.7%, and Team & Culture to 83.9% — all well above the prior cycle. The prior 90-Day data was not representative.
▲ 30-Day Overall improved 5.6 pts (78.8% → 83.5%)
The biggest single-wave improvement in the dataset. The prior dashboard showed a sharp 30-day dip — that has reversed. "Would recommend AssistRx" recovered from 74% to 79%, and "Made the right decision to join" rose from 81% to 86%.
▲ 30-Day Org Alignment reached 84.5% — strongest factor this wave
"I know how my work contributes to AssistRx goals" scores 91% favorable. New hires are entering with clearer line-of-sight to company objectives — potentially reflecting improved onboarding messaging.
● 60-Day Onboarding Experience slipped again (75.6% → 75.2%)
Despite improvements elsewhere, the 60-day retrospective view of onboarding has declined a further 0.4 pts. "Role expectations communicated effectively through onboarding" stays at 66% — the same weak floor as prior cycle. This specific item is not responding to changes made so far.
▼ 60-Day Overall dipped 0.8 pts (83.2% → 81.9%)
"I feel I belong at AssistRx" dropped from 78% to 77.4% at 60 days. Small movement but directionally worth watching — belonging is the highest-correlation driver of Overall in the 60 and 90-day data.
● "Discussed development goals with leadership" remains the floor — 50.9% at 60-Day, 53.8% at 90-Day
Persistent across every dashboard. No meaningful movement. This is the single item most in need of a structural intervention — manager accountability cadence, calendar prompt, or 90-day development conversation requirement.
▲ 30-Day recruitment accuracy improved: 66.7% → 66.7% (flat, but unfavorable dropped)
The "provided accurate information during recruitment" item held at 66.7% favorable — but the unfavorable share dropped from 21% to 17%. Fewer new hires are actively dissatisfied with recruitment accuracy, even if the favorable rate hasn't moved yet.
▲ 90-Day "Onboarding prepared me for my role" improved significantly: 66% → 76.1%
The item that was previously the lowest 90-day score jumped 10 points. This is the most encouraging single data point in the refresh — and the clearest signal that recent onboarding curriculum changes are having an effect by the time employees hit the 90-day mark.
