Top Movers — What's Changed
▼ 30-Day Onboarding Experience dropped 2.7 pts (84.4% → 81.7%)
Decline driven primarily by "I was provided accurate information about AssistRx during recruitment" falling from 70% to 66% favorable, with unfavorable jumping from 14% to 21%. New hires increasingly feel the recruitment pitch doesn't match reality.
▼ 30-Day Overall fell 5.2 pts (83.9% → 78.7%)
Largest single drop on the Overall factor. "Would recommend AssistRx as a great place to work" dropped from 82% to 74% favorable — a notable softening in advocacy at the early-tenure mark.
▼ 30-Day Job Satisfaction dropped 1.2 pts, but training got worse
"Efficient training on procedures applicable to my role" dropped from 65% to 59% favorable — already the lowest item, now even lower. Training effectiveness continues to be a structural weak point at the 30-day mark.
▼ 30-Day Team & Culture dropped 5.0 pts (85.7% → 80.6%)
All three Team & Culture items declined: "Felt welcomed" (86%→80%), "Team members supportive" (86%→81%), "Comfortable asking questions" (85%→80%). Culture was previously the strongest factor — this weakening warrants attention.
▲ 60-Day Overall improved 0.7 pts (82.5% → 83.2%)
"Would recommend AssistRx" jumped from 82% to 85% at the 60-day mark, and "I am proud to work at AssistRx" reached 85% favorable. By month two, sentiment is strengthening rather than declining.
▲ 60-Day Job Satisfaction improved 2.3 pts (77.2% → 79.5%)
"On-the-job training prepared me to do my job well" climbed from 67% to 72% favorable at 60 days — meaningful improvement in training perception once people are actually doing the work.
● Onboarding Checklist gaps largely improved
"High-quality 1:1 with leadership" dropped from 51% gap to 48% at 30-day, 51% to 35% at 60-day. "Equipped with tools/software" improved at every wave. Manager 1:1 time is still the #1 gap, but the trend line is finally moving in the right direction.
ⓘ 90-Day data is from a 2025 cohort, n=20
The 90-Day file covers Jan–Apr 2025 with only 20 respondents. Year-over-year comparison shows Job Satisfaction rebound from 83.8% to 78.3% and Communication weakening (74.5% → 73.3%), but with such a small sample these movements may not be statistically meaningful.
▲ Response rates are stronger this cycle
30-Day participation jumped from 41.7% to 45.5% — the highest rate observed across both dashboards. This may reflect either improved survey reminders or increased new-hire engagement.
